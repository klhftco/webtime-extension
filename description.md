Take control of your screen time. WebTime tracks how long you spend on each website, lets you set daily limits, and blocks distracting sites when you've hit them — no accounts, no cloud sync, all data stays on your device.

Time tracking
Automatically tracks time on the active, focused tab by site hostname. Tracking pauses when your screen is locked or device is suspended. Passive activity like reading or watching always counts. Counts "pickups" — how many times you navigate to a site from a different domain.

Daily limits & site blocking
Set per-site daily time limits. Add sites to a block list for immediate enforcement. Schedule block windows to restrict sites during specific hours. When a site hits its limit or block window, it is replaced with a blocked screen.

Usage data
View a daily pie-chart breakdown in the popup and a full weekly stacked-bar chart in settings. Scroll back through up to 4 weeks of history and compare any week against the previous week's usage. Export your usage data as JSON, or clear it entirely with a confirmation phrase. Clear individual days directly from the weekly view.

Settings protection
Protect your settings with an optional PIN or a slow-mode cooldown that requires a waiting period before changes take effect. Export and import your limits configuration as a backup.

Experimental
An optional tracking mode is available in settings that counts time across all visible windows, not just the focused one. This feature is under active development and behavior may change.

All data is stored locally on your device using Chrome's built-in storage. Nothing is sent to any server.
